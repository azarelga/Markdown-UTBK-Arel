1.  $\int t \ sin(t^2) dt$
	Misalkan $u =$ $t^2$ 
	$\frac{du}{dt} = 2t$
	$dt = \frac{du}{2t}$

	$\int t sin(t^{2}) \frac{d(t^{2})}{2t}$
	$= \frac{1}{2} \int sin(t^{2})d(t^{2})$
	$= \frac{1}{2} (-cos(t^{2}) + C)$
	$\therefore \int t sin(t^{2}) dt= - \frac{1}{2}cos(t^{2}) + C$

	$= \int tan^{2} t \ dt$
